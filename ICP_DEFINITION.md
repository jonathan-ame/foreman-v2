# Foreman v2 - Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

## Overview
Foreman v2 is an AI agent orchestration platform that enables businesses to deploy and manage AI agents without technical expertise. Our ICP represents the core audience most likely to experience immediate value and achieve success with our platform.

## Primary ICP: Tech-Aware Solopreneurs

**Demographics:**
- Company Size: 1-person businesses (solopreneurs)
- Revenue: $50k - $500k annual revenue
- Industry: Digital services, consulting, SaaS, content creation, e-commerce
- Location: US/North America primary, English-speaking markets

**Psychographics:**
- Technically curious but not deeply technical
- Time-strapped with multiple operational responsibilities
- Growth-focused; actively seeking efficiency tools
- Comfortable with SaaS tools but wary of complex technical setups
- Early adopters of AI tools but frustrated by fragmentation

**Pain Points:**
- Wearing too many hats (CEO, marketer, sales, operations)
- Limited bandwidth for repetitive tasks
- Can't afford full-time technical staff
- Overwhelmed by AI tool complexity and integration
- Need consistent business operations but lack processes

**Goals with Foreman:**
- Delegate operational tasks to AI agents
- Achieve consistent business execution
- Scale operations without adding headcount
- Maintain quality control without micromanaging
- Focus on high-value strategic work

## Secondary ICP: Small Digital Agencies & Consultancies

**Demographics:**
- Company Size: 2-10 employees
- Revenue: $200k - $2M annual revenue  
- Industry: Digital marketing, web development, consulting, creative services
- Team Structure: Founder/owner + small team of specialists

**Psychographics:**
- Service-focused with project-based work
- Constantly balancing client delivery with business growth
- Seeking operational efficiency across projects
- Value standardization and process improvement
- Aware of AI potential but uncertain about implementation

**Pain Points:**
- Inconsistent service delivery quality
- Manual processes eat into profit margins
- Difficulty scaling service capacity
- Client communication and project management overhead
- Limited technical resources for custom automation

**Goals with Foreman:**
- Standardize service delivery with AI assistance
- Improve client communication consistency
- Scale service capacity without proportional hiring
- Reduce operational overhead per project
- Maintain quality benchmarks across team members

## Technical Readiness Assessment

### Ready to Adopt (Green Signals)
- Already uses 3+ SaaS tools regularly
- Has basic understanding of LLMs/AI tools
- Comfortable with API concepts (even if not implementing)
- Uses project management tools (Asana, ClickUp, Trello)
- Regular internet user with cloud-first mindset

### Potential Barriers (Red Flags)
- No technical team member whatsoever
- Resistance to recurring software costs
- Minimal digital workflow maturity
- Expects fully autonomous "set and forget" solutions
- Unrealistic expectations about AI capabilities

## Acquisition Signals

**Strong Signals:**
- Searching for "AI assistant for small business"
- Evaluating CrewAI, SmythOS, other agent platforms
- Participating in AI tool communities/forums
- Following AI automation influencers/experts
- Previous experience with RPA or workflow automation

**Behavioral Signals:**
- Downloads/uses multiple productivity apps
- Subscribed to business/tech newsletters
- Attends virtual business growth events
- Engages with SaaS review platforms (G2, Capterra)

## Success Criteria (What Makes Them Successful with Foreman)

**Onboarding Success:**
- First agent provisioned within 15 minutes
- Completes one meaningful task in first 24 hours
- Sets up at least 2 recurring agent routines
- Integrates with 1-2 existing tools/workflows

**30-Day Retention:**
- Uses Foreman at least 3x per week
- Has active agents across multiple use cases
- Shows increased business efficiency metrics
- Refers to Foreman as "my AI team member"

**Expansion Signals:**
- Adds second/third agent types
- Upgrades to higher model tier
- Expands agent usage to new business areas
- Becomes reference customer/advocate

## Anti-ICP (Who We Should NOT Target Initially)

**Large Enterprises (100+ employees):**
- Require enterprise security/compliance features
- Need dedicated support and customization
- Have existing technical teams for custom solutions
- Require complex integration with legacy systems

**AI Developers/Technical Teams:**
- Prefer building custom solutions with LangChain/CrewAI
- Require deep technical control and customization
- Have in-house ML/AI engineering capabilities
- Seek open-source, self-hosted alternatives

**Non-Technical, Traditional Businesses:**
- Limited digital workflow maturity
- Resistance to AI/automation adoption
- Require extensive hand-holding and training
- Low willingness to pay for software tools

## Market Sizing Estimate

**US Market Addressable:**
- Solopreneurs: ~20M (based on self-employment statistics)
- Small Businesses (1-10 employees): ~5M businesses
- Initial Target Penetration (Year 1): 0.1% of addressable market

**Global Expansion Potential:**
- English-speaking markets: UK, Canada, Australia, NZ
- Tech-forward markets: Singapore, Israel, Germany
- Requires localized support and documentation
