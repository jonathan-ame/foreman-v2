# Foreman v2 - Go-to-Market Timeline (12-Month Plan)

## Overview
This document outlines the 12-month go-to-market rollout plan for Foreman v2, aligned with Phase 1-3 of the marketing roadmap from the GTM audit.

## Timeline Summary

**Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**  
Launch readiness, positioning validation, initial user acquisition

**Phase 2: Validation (Months 4-6)**  
Private beta scaling, channel testing, case study development

**Phase 3: Scaling (Months 7-12)**  
Public launch, paid acquisition scaling, partnership expansion

## Detailed Timeline

### **Month 1: Positioning & Launch Prep**

**Week 1-2: Finalize Foundation**
- ✅ Complete positioning documents (ICP, value prop, competitive analysis)
- ✅ Finalize brand voice guidelines
- 🚧 Complete investor materials (pitch deck, one-pager)
- **Owner:** CMO

**Week 3-4: Marketing Site Launch**
- Launch marketing website MVP
- Implement basic analytics (Google Analytics, heatmaps)
- Set up email collection (waitlist/early access)
- **Owner:** CTO/UX with CMO direction

**Key Deliverables Month 1:**
- Positioning documents complete
- Marketing site live
- Initial content calendar created
- Email collection system operational

### **Month 2: Private Beta Launch**

**Week 1-2: Beta Program Setup**
- Recruit 20 design partners from waitlist
- Create onboarding materials & documentation
- Set up feedback collection system
- **Owner:** CMO with CEO support

**Week 3-4: Beta Kickoff & Iteration**
- Onboard first 10 design partners
- Collect initial feedback & usage data
- Begin weekly check-ins with beta users
- **Owner:** CEO/CTO for product, CMO for feedback

**Key Deliverables Month 2:**
- 20 design partners recruited
- Beta onboarding materials complete
- Initial product feedback collected
- First iteration based on feedback

### **Month 3: Content Foundation & SEO**

**Week 1-2: Content Creation**
- Launch blog with 5 foundational articles

- Begin SEO optimization for target keywords
- **Owner:** CMO (hire content specialist if budget allows)

**Week 3-4: Channel Testing**
- Test 2-3 acquisition channels (LinkedIn, Reddit, communities)
- Begin partnership outreach to SMB tool providers
- Set up basic paid ads for testing
- **Owner:** CMO

**Key Deliverables Month 3:**
- Blog live with initial content
- SEO foundation established
- Channel test results analyzed
- First partnership conversations started

### **Month 4: Beta Expansion & Optimization**

**Week 3-4: Case Study Development**

- **Owner:** CMO

**Key Deliverables Month 4:**

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### **Monthly Tracking:**
1. **User Acquisition:** New users, CAC, channel performance
2. **Activation:** Time to first value, onboarding completion
3. **Engagement:** Weekly active users, tasks completed, feature usage
4. **Retention:** Monthly churn, expansion revenue, NPS
5. **Revenue:** MRR, ARR, ARPU, LTV:CAC ratio

### **Quarterly Goals:**
- **Q1:** Marketing site launch, 20 beta users, positioning validated
- **Q2:** 100 beta users, channel testing complete, case studies
- **Q3:** Public launch, 400 active users, $75k ARR
- **Q4:** 1,000 active users, $250k ARR, ready for Series A

## Resource Requirements

### **Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Current Team**
- **CEO:** Strategy, partnerships, fundraising
- **CTO:** Product development, technical oversight
- **CMO:** GTM strategy, marketing execution

### **Phase 2 (Months 4-6): First Hire Consideration**
- **Content Specialist:** Content creation, SEO, social media
- **Budget:** $40-60k/year

### **Phase 3 (Months 7-12): Additional Hires (Series A)**
- **Growth Marketer:** Paid acquisition, funnel optimization
- **Customer Success:** Onboarding, retention, support
- **Additional Engineering:** Product expansion

## Risk Mitigation

### **Market Risks:**
- **Slow adoption:** Focus on clear ROI messaging, case studies
- **Competitive entry:** Build strong community & network effects
- **Economic downturn:** Emphasize cost savings value prop

### **Execution Risks:**
- **Channel failure:** Test multiple channels simultaneously
- **Product gaps:** Close feedback loop with beta users
- **Team capacity:** Prioritize ruthlessly, consider contractors

### **Financial Risks:**
- **Higher CAC:** Focus on organic channels first
- **Lower ARPU:** Premium positioning, value-based pricing
- **Longer runway needed:** Conservative financial planning

## Success Criteria

### **Phase 1 Success (Month 3):**
- Marketing site converting at >5% to waitlist
- 20 engaged beta users providing actionable feedback
- Positioning resonating with target audience

### **Phase 2 Success (Month 6):**
- 100+ beta users with >70% weekly activity
- 2-3 channels showing <$500 CAC potential
- Clear path to $200+ ARPU validated

### **Phase 3 Success (Month 12):**
- 1,000+ active paying users
- $250k+ ARR
- <$500 CAC with >8:1 LTV:CAC ratio
- Ready for Series A funding

- Theme: "Are we ready for the next level?"
