# Foreman v2 - Brand Voice & Messaging Guidelines

## Overview
This document defines the Foreman brand voice, tone, and messaging framework to ensure consistent communication across all customer-facing channels and touchpoints.

## Core Brand Identity

### **Brand Essence:**  
**"The trusted partner for small business growth through AI"**

### **Mission Statement:**  
To empower solopreneurs and small businesses with AI capabilities that were previously only accessible to large enterprises with technical teams.

### **Vision:**  
A world where every small business has an AI team working alongside them, enabling sustainable growth and greater impact.

## Voice & Tone Framework

### **Primary Voice: Trusted Advisor**
- **Persona:** Experienced business consultant who understands small business challenges
- **Goal:** Build confidence while demystifying AI technology
- **Emotional Resonance:** Reassurance, clarity, partnership

### **Secondary Voice: Practical Innovator**
- **Persona:** Tech-savvy friend who explains complex concepts simply
- **Goal:** Make AI feel accessible, not intimidating
- **Emotional Resonance:** Curiosity, empowerment, optimism

## Voice Dimensions

### **1. Professional but Approachable**
- **Do:** Use business terminology but explain simply
- **Don't:** Use academic/technical jargon without context
- **Example:** "AI agent orchestration" → "AI team coordination"

### **2. Confident but Humble**
- **Do:** State capabilities clearly without overpromising
- **Don't:** Make unrealistic claims about AI capabilities
- **Example:** "Our agents handle routine business tasks" → not "Our agents will run your entire business"

### **3. Innovative but Practical**
- **Do:** Focus on business outcomes, not just technology
- **Don't:** Get lost in technical specifications
- **Example:** "Save 10+ hours per week" → not "Uses GPT-4 with 128k context"

### **4. Supportive but Direct**
- **Do:** Acknowledge challenges while offering solutions
- **Don't:** Dismiss customer concerns or difficulties
- **Example:** "AI setup can feel overwhelming, which is why we've made it simple" → not "Anyone can do this, it's easy"

- **Tone:** Engaging, conversational, insightful

## Messaging Architecture

### **Core Messaging Hierarchy:**

1. **Problem Statement:** "Small business owners struggle with limited bandwidth and lack technical expertise for AI"
2. **Solution Statement:** "Foreman provides no-code AI agents with managed operations"
3. **Value Proposition:** "Scale your business with AI, not headcount"
4. **Proof Points:** "15-minute setup, business-ready agents, growth-aligned pricing"
5. **Call to Action:** "Start your AI team today"

### **Message Pillars:**

**Pillar 1: Simplicity**
- **Core Message:** "AI made simple for small business"
- **Supporting Messages:** No coding required, 15-minute setup, intuitive interface
- **Evidence:** Step-by-step onboarding, pre-configured agents

**Pillar 2: Reliability**
- **Core Message:** "AI you can trust with your business"
- **Supporting Messages:** Managed operations, quality guarantees, human oversight
- **Evidence:** Built on Paperclip/OpenClaw, 99.9% uptime, transparent operations

**Pillar 3: Growth**
- **Core Message:** "Scale smart with AI assistance"
- **Supporting Messages:** Start small, grow as needed, positive ROI
- **Evidence:** Tiered pricing, BYOK option, customer success metrics

## Writing Guidelines

### **Word Choice & Terminology:**

**Preferred Terms:**
- AI agents (not "bots" or "robots")
- Business operations (not "tasks")
- Your AI team (emphasize partnership)
- Simple setup (not "easy" - sets realistic expectations)
- Consistent quality (not "perfect" - sets realistic expectations)
- Growth together (emphasize partnership)

**Terms to Avoid:**
- Magic, magical, wizardry (overpromises)
- Revolutionary, disruptive (hyperbolic)
- Free forever (unrealistic)
- Set and forget (misleading about AI)
- Perfect, flawless (unrealistic)

### **Sentence Structure:**
- **Use:** Active voice, short sentences, clear transitions
- **Avoid:** Passive voice, complex clauses, jargon without explanation
- **Example:** "Your AI agent handles customer inquiries" → not "Customer inquiries are handled by your AI agent"

### **Formatting & Readability:**
- **Headlines:** Benefit-focused, 6-8 words max
- **Body Copy:** Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), bullet points for lists
- **CTAs:** Action-oriented, clear value promise
- **Readability Target:** 8th-10th grade level (Flesch-Kincaid)

## Persona-Specific Messaging

### **For Solopreneurs:**
- **Focus:** Time savings, wearing multiple hats, cost-effectiveness
- **Voice:** Supportive, understanding of hustle
- **Example:** "Get back the hours you spend on admin work"

### **For Small Business Owners:**
- **Focus:** Scaling operations, team consistency, quality control
- **Voice:** Strategic, business-partner oriented
- **Example:** "Scale your service capacity without proportional hiring"

### **For Technically-Curious Business Owners:**
- **Focus:** AI capabilities, integration potential, future possibilities
- **Voice:** Knowledgeable but accessible
- **Example:** "Leverage enterprise-grade AI infrastructure without the complexity"

## Quality Standards & Review

### **Brand Voice Checklist:**
- [ ] Uses "trusted advisor" tone
- [ ] Focuses on business outcomes, not just technology
- [ ] Avoids jargon without explanation
- [ ] Sets realistic expectations
- [ ] Includes clear next steps
- [ ] Maintains consistent terminology

### **Approval Workflow:**
1. **Content Creation:** Follow brand voice guidelines
2. **Peer Review:** Check for consistency and clarity
3. **CMO Review:** Ensure alignment with positioning
4. **Legal Review:** For terms, guarantees, compliance
5. **CEO Approval:** For major launches, pricing changes

- **Support Analysis:** Common questions indicate messaging gaps

### **Iteration Schedule:**
- **Monthly:** Review customer feedback for voice adjustments
- **Quarterly:** Update messaging based on market changes
- **Annually:** Comprehensive brand voice review and refresh

### **Documentation Updates:**

- Document successful messaging examples
- Update guidelines based on A/B test results
